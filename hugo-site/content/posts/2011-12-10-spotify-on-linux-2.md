Hey nerds. Guess what? Spotify on Linux. Super easy too...fire up a terminal \[ctrl-alt-t\] Step one: **cd /etc/apt**Step two: **sudo vim sources.list**Step three: add "**deb http://repository.spotify.com stable non-free**" to the file \[caption id="attachment\_4147" align="aligncenter" width="499" caption="sudo vim"\][![spotify for linux](/uploads/2011/12/spotify-one-499x570.png "spotify one")](/uploads/2011/12/spotify-one.png)\[/caption\] Step four: **sudo apt-get update**\[caption id="attachment\_4148" align="aligncenter" width="489" caption="\_{\_n\_/\_Y\_}\_Y"\][![spotify linux ubuntu](/uploads/2011/12/spotify_linux-489x570.png "spotify_linux")](/uploads/2011/12/spotify_linux.png)\[/caption\] Step five: **sudo apt-get install spotify-client-qt**\[caption id="attachment\_4149" align="aligncenter" width="570" caption="can I kick it"\][![spotify on ubuntu](/uploads/2011/12/linux_spotify-570x356.png "linux_spotify")](/uploads/2011/12/linux_spotify.png)\[/caption\] That is that. It is not a supported version, but I doubt anyone installing via aptitude will really care about that item. Side thought: How sad is it that my favorite album came out in 1993? Old balls.
